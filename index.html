Why is EO data still so hard to use?

Despite a sky full of satellites beaming back a firehose of information — imagery, radar, thermal, atmospheric sensors — most Earth data sits unused. It’s trapped behind incompatible formats, fragmented catalogs, and hard-to-scale infrastructure.

Organizations face faimiliar pain points:

  • Fragmented sources: EO data comes from dozens of constellations, each with their own quirks.
  • Heavy processing: Whether it's cloud masks, georeferencing, or radar scan conversion, the burden falls on the user.
  • Specialist silos: Remote sensing, GIS, and ML expertise are scarce — and often required just to ask simple questions.
  • Slow paths to insight: Turning complex sensor readings into useful signals takes too long.

EO data should empower more than just a handful of technical teams. StratimAI is here to change that.

All sensors. One API.

StratimAI unifies optical imagery, radar, thermal, atmospheric, and more — abstracting away the complexity of sourcing, transforming, and analyzing satellite data. No more decoding EPSG codes, querying obscure metadata, or setting up your own cloud pipelines. Just ask for the insight you need, and get structured results you can trust.

From data to decision

StratimAI doesn’t just serve data — it runs the analysis too. Our backend processes every EO stream through prebuilt, extensible AI workflows: detecting changes, classifying land and water use, measuring vegetation health, estimating flood risk, mapping urban growth, and more.

Behind the scenes, we handle source fusion, cloud masking, terrain correction, and even cross-sensor harmonization. Insights arrive as ready-to-use JSON, GeoTIFF, CSV, or GeoJSON — built to drop into your application or data stack.

And it’s not a black box. Want to control thresholds? Use your own model? Fine-tune the spatial granularity? You can. That’s what we call data-to-decision sovereignty.
